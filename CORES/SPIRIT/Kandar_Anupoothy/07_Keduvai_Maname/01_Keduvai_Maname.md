
## **“கெடுவாய் மனனே” — what is Arunagirinathar really saying?**

In the opening line of _Kandar Anubhuti_,  
**“கெடுவாய் மனனே”** is often translated as _“O spoiled mind”_.

This is **not an insult** and not moral condemnation.  
It is a **diagnosis**.

Arunagirinathar is stating a fact about the **default tendency of the human mind**.

---

## 1. “Spoiled” does NOT mean evil

Here, _கெடுவாய்_ does **not** mean:

- sinful
    
- bad
    
- malicious
    

It means:

- **easily corrupted**
    
- **unstable**
    
- **prone to distortion**
    
- **quick to slip away from truth**
    

Think of it as:

> _A mind that does not stay clean on its own._

Left alone, it **bends**, **drifts**, and **decays**.

---

## 2. Why the mind tends toward spoilage

Human thoughts generated by the mind naturally tend toward _கெடுதல்_ (spoilage) because:

### ① The mind seeks comfort over truth

It prefers:

- avoidance over engagement
    
- ease over clarity
    
- safety over honesty
    

### ② The mind protects identity

It constantly asks:

- “How do I look?”
    
- “Am I safe?”
    
- “Am I winning or losing?”
    

This self-protection bends perception.

### ③ The mind multiplies narratives

One sensation → many stories → confusion.

Reality is simple; the mind is not.

### ④ The mind avoids direct contact

Instead of _touching_ reality, it:

- postpones
    
- distracts
    
- intellectualizes
    
- escapes
    

This avoidance is the **root of spoilage**.

So yes — **human thought, by default, tends toward spoilage** unless checked.

---

## 3. The crucial link: “கெடுவாய் மனனே” → “கரவாது இடுவாய்”

This is not accidental placement in the poem.

Arunagirinathar first says:

> **“The mind is spoiled by default.”**

Then he prescribes the medicine:

> **“Give effort without hiding.”**

Why?

Because **hidden effort is exactly how the spoiled mind operates**.

A spoiled mind:

- starts but doesn’t enter
    
- works but withholds presence
    
- acts while keeping an escape route
    
- pretends to let go, but actually avoids
    

So _கரவாது இடுவாய்_ is not advice to be generous —  
it is a **discipline to counter mental decay**.

---

## 4. Very important clarification (don’t miss this)

Arunagirinathar is **not saying**:

> “Destroy the mind.”

He is saying:

> **“Do not trust the mind’s first movement.”**

The first impulse of the mind is usually:

- avoidance
    
- distortion
    
- self-protection
    

That is _கெடுவாய்_.

The correction is **not suppression**, but **honest action**.

---

## 5. What this means in lived terms

When a task appears:

- The **spoiled mind** says:  
    _“Too hard… later… let’s rest… let’s think more.”_
    
- **Unhidden effort** says:  
    _“Let me touch this fully for a few minutes.”_
    

That single honest entry **cleans the mind**.

Not permanently — but **for that moment**.

And that is enough.

---

## 6. Final truth (this ties everything together)

The mind **will** spoil.  
That is its nature.

Arunagirinathar is not asking you to perfect the mind.

He is showing you **how not to obey it blindly**.

> When effort is given without hiding,  
> the spoiled mind loses its power —  
> not because it is defeated,  
> but because it is no longer followed.

That is the quiet genius of _கெடுவாய் மனனே_.
